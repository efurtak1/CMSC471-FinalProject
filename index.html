Fatal Trends of America

By: Elizabeth Furtak, William Yang, and Peyton Chang.

This webpage features an interactive visualization of deaths in the U.S., displaying trends by cause and geographic region. The main view includes a heatmap that highlights mortality rates across states, allowing users to explore variations over time using a year slider. Additionally, an interactive graph with bouncing balls represents different causes of death, where each ball's movement and size correspond to mortality data. Users can filter by year, cause, and region to uncover patterns and trends in the dataset.

Mortality Trends Over Time Geographically

This visualization shows how mortality rates have changed across the United States. The goal of this visualization is to detect if any particular illness is more prevalent in certain geographical areas or in certain years. First, we implemented a dropdown and slider to filter data across cause of death and year. We also employed a red color gradient to differentiate the number of deaths in each state. Lastly, we added a mouseover feature to showcase more specific details such as death rate per state. To utilize this visualization, begin by choosing a cause of death from the dropdown as well as a year, and begin observing.

Interactive Mortality Trends Over Time

This visualization displays mortality amount through a packed bubble plot. The goal of this visualization is to reveal the leading causes of death in total, without the lens of geography. The packed bubble plot separates each cause of death into an individual bubble with the size correlating to the number of deaths. Similar to the geographical visualization, there is the ability to filter the data by year with the slider and hovering on a bubble showcases more detail. Another exciting feature this visualization supports is the ability to move the bubbles around. Just move the bubbles around and watch them come back.
